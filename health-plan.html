Daily Walking Protocol
```
After Every Meal
Walk in place for 5–10 minutes within 30 minutes of finishing eating.
This is your single highest-impact habit. Muscles absorb glucose directly — no insulin needed.
Every Hour
2–5 minute walk in place during your workday, every hour on the hour.
Set a phone alarm. Just standing and pacing counts. This keeps circulation going and insulin from spiking between meals.
Post-Dinner
10–15 minute walk after dinner — outside if possible.
Evening is when blood sugar tends to sit high. A walk here dramatically lowers overnight glucose.
Weekly Total
Aim for 7,000–9,000 steps/day total across all movement.
You don't need a dedicated "cardio session" — accumulated steps throughout the day are just as effective.
```

```
📈 When to Increase — The Rule
Step 1 Complete all sets and reps with good form and it feels manageable (not easy, but doable) for 2 days in a row.
Step 2 Add 2 reps per set first. Do that until it's manageable again for 2 sessions.
Step 3 Then add 1 more set (up to 4 sets max per exercise).
Step 4 Once at 4×15 and it's still easy — move to the harder variation listed for that exercise and start back at 3×8.
Never increase more than one thing at a time. Soreness is fine — sharp pain is not. Rest that muscle if needed.
```

⚡
Non-Negotiable Daily Rules
🥩
Protein at every meal
Aim for 40–50g per meal. This is the single biggest lever for staying full and keeping insulin stable.
🚫
No liquid sugar
Juice, soda, sweetened coffee, sports drinks — these spike blood sugar faster than almost anything else.
⏱️
3 meals, no constant snacking
Insulin needs to come back down between meals. Eating every 2 hours keeps it elevated all day.
😴
7–8 hours of sleep
One bad night raises blood sugar and insulin resistance measurably. Sleep is not optional for this goal.
🥗
Vegetables before carbs
Fiber first slows glucose absorption. Start every meal with vegetables or salad, always.
🌙
Low carb after 5 PM
Insulin sensitivity drops in the evening. Keep dinner protein and vegetable heavy, minimal starch.
What to Expect Week by Week
1
Week 1–2: Water weight drops fast (2–5 lbs). Blood sugar stabilizes noticeably. Energy may dip briefly as your body adjusts — this is normal.
2
Week 3–4: True fat loss begins. Hunger settles — high protein keeps cravings low. Strength training starts feeling easier as your body adapts.
3
Month 2+: Steady 0.5–1 lb/week fat loss. Muscle builds underneath. Metabolic rate improves. This is where the compound effect kicks in.
4
The goal (150 lbs): You've done this before — your body knows the way. Consistency is the only variable. The plan handles everything else.
